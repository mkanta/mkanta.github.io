2.1. Forces

  1. Why does a child on a toboggan fall backward when the toboggan is given a sharp tug? (2 marks)
  2. If you get to your feet in a canoe and move towards the front, the canoe moves in the opposite direction. Explain why. (2 marks)
  3. A net force of \(2.5 \times 10^3\) N acts on an object for \(5.0\) s. During this time, the object accelerates from rest to a velocity of \(48\) km/h. What is the mass of the object? (\(9.4 \times 10^2\)kg) (2 marks)
  4. Atwood’s Pulley: A \(3.0\)kg mass is attached to a \(5.0\)kg mass by a string that passes over a frictionless pulley. Find the following values when the masses are allowed to hang freely:
    1. acceleration of the system (\(2.5\)\(\,\text{m}/\text{s}^2\)) (2 marks)
    2. magnitude of the tension in the string (\(37\) N) (2 marks)
  5. A \(9.6\)kg sled is pulled along firmly packed snow (which is level). If the coefficient of kinetic friction between the surfaces is \(0.11\), what is the magnitude of the force of friction acting on the sled? (10 N) (1 mark)
  6. A \(20.0\,\)N box is placed on the floor. A force of \(3.0\,\)N is required to keep the object moving at a constant speed. Find the coefficient of kinetic friction between the two surfaces. (\(0.15\)) (2 marks)
  7. An \(8.0\,\)g bullet traveling at \(400\,\)m/s passes through a heavy block i of wood in \(4.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\)s, emerging with a velocity of \(100\,\)m/s. Ignore any motion of the wood.
    1. With what average force did the wood oppose the motion of the bullet? (\(-6.0 \times 10^3\,\)N) (2 marks)
    2. How thick is the wood? (\(1.0\times 10^{-1}\,\)m) (2 marks)
  8. Why is the coefficient of kinetic friction always smaller than the coefficient of static friction? (2 marks)
  9. Fletcher’s Trolley: A \(5.0\,\)kg mass rests on a level frictionless table and is attached to a \(3.0\,\)kg mass by a string that passes over a frictionless pulley. Find the tension in the string when the masses are released. (\(18\,\)N) (3 marks)
  10. Fletcher’s Trolley: A steel block rests on a level table and is attached to a 5.0 kg mass by a string that passes over a frictionless pulley. There is 24 N of friction present between the steel block and the table. If the acceleration of the system is 2.9 m/s2, find the mass of the steel block. (3.6 kg) (3 marks)
  11. The diagram shows a \(1.20\,\)kg cart on top of a table attached to two masses by strings going over frictionless pulleys. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the cart and the table is \(0.140\). If the mass of m is 1.00 kg and the mass of M is 2.00 kg, what is the acceleration of the cart? (1.94 m/s2) (3 marks)
  12. A \(10\,\)kg block on a \(30^\circ\) slope. If the coefficient of friction is \(0.30\) determine the following:
    1. the force of friction. (25 N) (1 mark)
    2. the force of gravity parallel to the slope. (49 N) (1 mark)
    3. the acceleration of the block down the slope. (2.4 m/s2) (1 mark)
    4. The minimum coefficient of friction needed to prevent the block from sliding. (\(0.58\)) (1 mark)
  13. If a stationary rock climber with a mass of \(70.0\,\)kg is suspended so that the rope makes an angle \(10.0\circ\) from the vertical. Determine the tension in the rope. (676 N) (2 marks) The tension is keeping the climber in place so it counteracts the component of the force due to gravity in the \(10^\circ\) direction from the vertical. Therefore, \[F_T=mg\cos 10^\circ=70\times 9.81\times\cos 10^\circ=6.76267483994\times 10^2,\] which rounds to \(676\,\)N.
